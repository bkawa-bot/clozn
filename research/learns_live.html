clozn — a local runtime where the model's interior is legible.
What's real here. The model is frozen the whole time; "teach" fits a small soft prefix by a few gradient steps (test-time training) — the only thing that changes. "Apply" is the frozen model's own output with that prefix in play, shown against its baseline with no prefix. "Verify" takes the model's stated sentence and applies it with no prefix on held-out words, scoring it against the true answers, the prefix's own behavior (agreement), an oracle (the hand-written true rule, a soundness check), and an averaged wrong-rule baseline the statement must clear. The model can state a rule clumsily or wrongly; that's why the words are checked, not trusted.
